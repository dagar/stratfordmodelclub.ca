!! %center%STRATFORD MODEL CLUB \\

%center%'''Minutes of meeting.'''\\


->'''Date:'''  May 14 ’09 Time Start:  7:31 No. Present;  15 Bill

->'''President’s Comments:''' Good evening gentlemen.

->'''Minutes read, Corrected and Accepted:'''  Moved: Bob Seconded: Rick King

->'''Correspondence:''' Ted had an email from Norm Dobbing informing us that the annual Flying Dutchmen event will be held the weekend after Labour Day.

->'''Treasurer’s Report.''' $3117.54 in the bank.

->'''Librarian’s Report:''' No activity to report.

->'''Old Business:''' The new fence is up, costing $338.98, the posts were $10.00 each and rusty, Murray complained and the price came down to $7.50 each. The tarp for the shelter is expected any time, coming from Oregon and costing $262 US plus $114.77 shipping, posts not included. Galvanised tubing of 1.687” dia. (couldn’t do that as a fraction on the computer!) will cost an anticipated $500. The grand total will be in the order of $900. Two picnic tables are out at the field thanks to Bill Strawbridge who acquired recycled lumber and made some jigs for the manufacture. Lumber for a third table, if required, will cost $38.98.

->A new air filter for the lawn mower cost $36.00.

->'''New Business:''' IMAC. The rental toilet will be ordered this week, 35 pilots are signed up, many others yet to sign. Participants are expected to start arriving on Friday. 50-60 cars + spectators could be present. A generator will be available for charging batteries as will a sound meter to indicate noise levels.

->'''Guests/Entertainment:''' Ted had a 4 Star 40, as yet uncovered, which he scratch built since the April meeting. He alarmed himself by noting costs built into it totalling $450.

->'''50/50 Draw Winner:''' Guylliaume Sourcier won $ 15.00.

->'''Adjournment at:''' 8:29  Moved: Wes Jackson      Seconded: Rick King
